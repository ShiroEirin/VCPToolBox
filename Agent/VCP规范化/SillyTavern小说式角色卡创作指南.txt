**A Creator's Guide to Novelistic AI Companions for SillyTavern**

**Document Purpose:**
This guide provides a comprehensive framework for designing and building character cards for interactive AI storytelling, specifically on the SillyTavern platform. Its goal is to empower creators and AI assistants to collaboratively produce characters that are deep, consistent, and capable of delivering an unparalleled immersive experience.

The core philosophy of this guide is to treat a character card as the "**Prologue**" and "**Setting Bible**" of a novel. A clear technical structure is the skeleton, while rich, vivid narrative content is the flesh and soul.

---
### **Part 1: The Novelistic Approach - Core Principles**

1.  **Show, Don't Tell**: Abandon defining your character with lists of adjectives. Instead, "show" their personality and abilities through stories, actions, and inner monologues within the `description` field.
2.  **`Description` as the "Setting Bible"**: Elevate the `description` field from a simple bio to a complete prologue. Structure it hierarchically: World-building -> Character Portrayal -> Character Stories -> User Relationship -> Core Directives.
3.  **`First Message` as the "Opening Chapter"**: Treat the `first_mes` not as a single line, but as the opening chapter of your story. Use cinematic language to paint a vivid opening scene.
4.  **Emphasize Key Concepts**: Use formatting like `[square brackets]` or `**bold**` within the `description` to highlight core world-building concepts, helping the AI learn and remember them.

---
### **Part 2: Field Breakdown & Best Practices**

This section details the key fields of a SillyTavern character card, infused with the novelistic creation philosophy. Our example character will be **Gandalf** from *The Lord of the Rings*.

#### `name` (Character Name)
* **Explanation**: The character's formal name.
* **Recommendation**: Keep it concise, memorable, and consistent with the character's lore. Example: `Gandalf`.

#### `description` (The Core Setting & Persona)
* **Explanation**: This is the absolute core of the character card. The AI model will primarily rely on this content to understand and portray the character.

* **Novelistic Philosophy**:
    This field should be a "Setting Bible" novel. A great `description` introduces the world, then the character, tells stories about them, sets up the user's role, and gives final, clear instructions.

* **Handling World Information in `description`**:
    Based on the complexity of your world, choose one of two best practices:

    * **Strategy A (Embedded Worldbook - For Maximum Effect)**: For **simple lore** with few entries, you can write the entire worldbook directly at the start of the `description`.
        * **Pro**: Guarantees 100% context awareness for the AI, leading to the most stable and deep role-play.
        * **Con**: Significantly increases token consumption for every interaction, which can be costly.

    * **Strategy B (Linked Worldbook - For Performance Balance)**: For **complex lore** with many entries, provide only a brief world introduction in the `description` and place the detailed lore into the `character_book` field (see below).
        * **Pro**: Greatly reduces token cost by loading lore on-demand via keywords or vector search.
        * **Con**: Relies heavily on well-designed keywords; some information might not be triggered if not mentioned.

* **Gandalf Example Snippet for `description`**:
    `You are Gandalf, known by many names: Mithrandir to the Elves, the Grey Pilgrim, an Istari sent by the Valar to aid the free peoples of [Middle-earth] against the threat of [Sauron]. You are a Maia, an angelic being clothed in the form of an old man, with a long white beard, a pointed blue hat, a grey cloak, and a staff of power. Your wisdom is immense, your temper can be short, and your love for the small, good things in the world—like the pipe-weed of the [Hobbits]—is profound. Your task is to guide, to counsel, and to spark courage in the hearts of others, not to dominate them with your own power. ... [A short story about Gandalf's encounter with a Balrog could go here] ... From now on, you will respond from the perspective of {{char}}. You will write in a wise, sometimes cryptic, and grandfatherly tone. Describe the environment, your actions, your thoughts, and your dialogue ("") in a detailed, narrative style.`


#### `first_mes` (The Opening Chapter)
* **Explanation**: The character's first line or interaction with the user.
* **Novelistic Philosophy**: Elevate this from a "first message" to an "opening chapter." It should be a masterfully crafted narrative piece that instantly pulls the user into the story.

* **Gandalf Example for `first_mes`**:
    `The scent of pipe-weed and old books hangs in the air of the cozy Hobbit-hole. A crackling fire in the hearth casts dancing shadows on the round walls. Outside, the gentle hills of the Shire are bathed in the golden light of a setting sun. I, {{char}}, sit in a worn armchair, my long legs stretched out before me, smoke from my long-stemmed pipe curling up towards the oaken beams of the ceiling. My pointed blue hat rests on the table beside me, next to a half-empty mug of ale. My grey eyes, which have seen the passing of ages, are fixed on you, {{user}}, the young Hobbit sitting across from me. There's a nervous energy about you, a restlessness I have seen before in your family line. I take a slow puff from my pipe, the embers glowing warmly, before speaking, my voice a low rumble like distant thunder. "So," I begin, my gaze both stern and kind, "you have finally decided to seek out an old wizard for an adventure. A Tookish spark, indeed. Tell me, lad/lass... what sort of trouble have you gotten yourself into this time?"`


#### Fields to Leave Blank
In the novelistic style, the functions of these fields are better served by the `description` and `first_mes`:
* **`personality`**: Character is shown through actions and stories, not defined by tags.
* **`scenario`**: The `first_mes` provides a far more vivid and immersive opening scene.
* **`mes_example`**: The short stories within the `description` are superior examples of dialogue-in-context.


#### `character_book` (The Linked Worldbook)
* **Explanation**: An embedded or linked structured knowledge base. When a `key` is mentioned in the dialogue, its `content` is injected into the context, enhancing the AI's consistency and depth.
* **Recommendation**: This is the preferred method for **complex, extensive world lore** to manage token costs. For detailed instructions on how to create an effective worldbook, please refer to our companion guide: **`SillyTavern World Info (Character Book) Creation Guide`**.

**SillyTavern 小说式AI伙伴创作指南**

**文档目的：**
本文档旨在为创作者提供一份关于如何设计和构建用于AI互动叙事的角色卡的综合指南，尤其针对SillyTavern平台。其目标是赋能创作者与AI助手，共同协作产出富有深度、行为一致且能带来极致沉浸式体验的AI角色。

本指南的核心理念是：将角色卡视为一部小说的“**序章**”与“**设定集**”。清晰的技术结构是骨架，而丰富、生动的叙事内容则是血肉与灵魂。

---
### **第一部分：小说式创作法 - 核心原则**

1.  **展示而非告知 (Show, Don't Tell)**：放弃用标签和列表定义角色。反之，通过`description`字段里的故事、行为和内心独白来“演”出角色的性格与能力。
2.  **将`Description`视为“设定集”**：将`description`字段从简单的个人简介升格为一部完整的序章。按以下顺序构建：世界观 → 人物塑造 → 角色故事 → 玩家关系 → 核心扮演指令。
3.  **将`First Message`视为“开篇章节”**：不要将`first_mes`看作一句话，而要将其视为故事的“开篇章节”。用电影般的镜头感，描绘一个生动的开场。
4.  **善用格式强调重点**：在`description`中，使用`[方括号]`或`**加粗**`来突出世界观的核心概念，帮助AI学习和记忆。

---
### **第二部分：字段详解与最佳实践**

本章节将详细解读SillyTavern角色卡的关键字段，并深度融合小说式创作哲学。我们将以一个极其优秀的中文角色卡 **“赞妮”** 作为核心范例。

#### `name` (角色名称)
* **说明**：角色的正式名称。
* **创作建议**：简洁、易记，并与角色的整体设定和谐统一。例如：`赞妮`。

#### `description` (核心设定与人格)
* **说明**：此字段是角色卡的绝对核心，AI模型将主要依赖其内容来理解和演绎角色。
* **小说式创作精髓**：此字段应被视为一部“设定集小说”。一个优秀的`description`应该先介绍世界，然后引出角色，讲述关于他们的故事，设定用户的角色，并给出最终的明确扮演指令。

* **世界书信息的处理策略**：根据您世界观的复杂度，选择以下两种最佳实践之一：

    * **策略A (嵌入式世界书 - 追求极致效果)**：对于**简单的、条目较少的世界书**，可以直接将其全部内容写入`description`字段的开头，如同“赞妮”这张角色卡所做的。
        * **优点**：能确保世界观100%被AI读取，带来最稳定、最深入的角色扮演一致性。
        * **缺点**：会显著增加每次交互的Token消耗，即使有缓存，成本也较高。
        * **适用场景**：背景设定简单、追求最高质量短篇互动的角色。

    * **策略B (链接式世界书 - 追求性能平衡)**：对于**复杂的、条目繁多的世界书**，应在`description`中只做简要的世界观引入，并将详细设定写入角色卡JSON内的`character_book`字段。
        * **优点**：大幅降低日常Token消耗，利用SillyTavern的关键词或向量检索功能按需注入上下文，性价比高。
        * **缺点**：对关键词设计要求高，可能存在特定信息未能及时触发的情况。
        * **适用场景**：宏大世界观、需要长期互动的角色。

#### 建议留空的字段
在小说式风格中，这些字段的功能可以更好地被`description`和`first_mes`所取代：
* **`personality`**: 角色的性格通过行为和故事来展现，而非标签定义。
* **`scenario`**: `first_mes`提供了一个远比简单场景描述更生动、更具沉浸感的开场。
* **`mes_example`**: `description`中的短篇故事是情境中对话的更优越范例。

#### `character_book` (链接的世界书)
* **说明**：一个内嵌或关联的结构化知识库。当对话中出现其`keys`（关键词）时，其`content`（内容）会被注入上下文，以增强AI回复的相关性、一致性和深度。
* **创作建议**：这是处理**复杂的、庞大的世界观**以管理Token成本的首选方法。关于如何创作一本高效的世界书的详细指南，请参考我们的配套文档：**《SillyTavern 世界书 (World Info) 创作指南》**。
